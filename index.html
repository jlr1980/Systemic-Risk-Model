What the Substrate Carries

The substrate is the thing beneath the thing. It is the legal systems that enforce contracts, the energy systems that power logistics, the ecological systems that provide water and stable climate, the governance systems that maintain social order.

Most investment models treat the substrate as a constant. It is not. When the substrate degrades, diversification within the financial system reveals itself as correlated exposure to a single condition: institutional continuity.

The Substrate Health Index aggregates damage from active disruption events, background EROI decline, and time spent in stressed states. It is offset by stabilising vectors: successful energy transition and institutional formation. When the index falls below 50, the institutional cascade becomes the central risk rather than a tail risk.

Institutional Cascade Model
Physical risk alone is survivable. The amplifier is sequential institutional failure. Each step makes the next more likely and more severe.
Cascade Stages (Representative $100M Portfolio, 30 Years)
~22%
Physical risk alone. Climate impacts with institutions functioning.
~33%
Insurance retreat. Insurers withdraw. Uninsurable assets lose value. Risk socialises.
~52%
Municipal fiscal stress. Uninsured infrastructure degrades tax base. Services contract.
~71%
Sovereign contagion. Municipal stress aggregates to sovereign balance sheets. Credit reprices.
~86%
Full cascade. Correlated credit repricing across asset classes. Diversification fails.
The Amplifier

The gap between 22% and 86% is not additional physical risk. It is the cost of institutional entanglement.

Insulation repositions within the system. It does not reduce the probability of the cascade. Only substrate stabilisation addresses the source: investing in the institutional infrastructure that would prevent or absorb the cascade.

In practice, achieving a sub-20% probability requires setting most threat probabilities well below observable trend lines. That position requires active disbelief in current data.

This model makes beliefs testable. It does not make predictions. The base probabilities reflect observable 2026 trajectories and institutional research. They are starting points, not claims of certainty.

The model's purpose is to shift the burden of proof. Instead of arguing that the world is getting worse, it asks: show me the version of the next decade where you don't need to hedge. If you cannot construct that version from settings you believe in, the case for a different approach makes itself.

For those whose settings produce concern, the question becomes: where does capital go? The companion document, Worldview and Wealth Stewardship, walks through the upstream questions. The Perpetual Civilisational Treasury Protocol provides the operational architecture for deploying capital toward the institutional infrastructure this model shows is needed.

Key Design Decisions

Starting state: Managed Disruption. Insurance markets are already retreating, AI labour displacement is measurable, democratic institutions are under stress in multiple major economies. Users who disagree can adjust.

Nonlinear compounding. Three simultaneous disruptions produce more total pressure than one of equal combined magnitude.

Persistence differentiation. Acute shocks (pandemic, cyber) carry 2-year persistence. Structural shifts (insurance, democracy, AI) carry 8-10 years. This is the model's most consequential distinction.

Asymmetric stabilisation. Stabilisation uses a 0.5 coefficient vs 0.7 for disruption. Complex systems are easier to break than to repair.

EROI background constraint. Net energy decline applies gentle downward pressure on all transitions, increasing over the decade.

Recovery degradation. Time spent in stressed states progressively reduces recovery probability, creating a persistence trap.

Substrate Health Index. An aggregate measure of institutional, ecological, and governance capacity. Damaged by active events and EROI decline. Restored by stabilising vectors.

Limitations

Events fire independently. In reality, conflict amplifies cyber risk, democratic collapse worsens sovereign debt. The correlation matrix partially addresses this but does not implement full causal chains.

Static base transition matrix. Recovery capacity may degrade faster than the model implies. The recovery degradation function partially compensates.

Portfolio returns are simplified. Real performance depends on geographic concentration, sector exposure, leverage, and liquidity. The three archetypes are illustrative.

No geographic dimension. The model treats the world as one system. Substrate degradation is uneven across regions.

No portfolio-to-system feedback. In reality, portfolio crashes amplify systemic stress through forced selling and credit contraction.

Adaptation is not modelled. Policy response and human adaptive capacity could reduce impacts. The model assumes current trajectories, which is conservative by design.

5,000 simulations produce sampling noise (±2-3 percentage points). Re-roll to confirm pattern stability.
